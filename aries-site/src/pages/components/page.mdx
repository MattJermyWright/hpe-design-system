Page makes it easy to implement [page layouts](/templates/page-layouts) uniformly across HPE applications. Two components, Page and PageContent, work together to ensure HPE theme-defined margins, horizontal alignment, max-width, and responsive behaviors are applied in a consistent, turn-key manner.

## Guidance

Page is the outer layout component. Pageâ€™s `kind` determines which theme-defined padding, min/max-width, and alignment should be applied to its child PageContent components.

PageContent should be a child of Page. This allows PageContent to adhere to the min/max-width, padding, and alignment values set by its parent Page `kind`.

In most cases, all of the content for a page can be wrapped in a single PageContent component. However, if varying background colors are needed for different page sections or a full-width background color is needed
(link to variant below), then individual page sections can each be wrapped in their own PageContent.

There are 3 kinds of options for Page. 
1. [Wide (Default)](https://design-system.hpe.design/templates/page-layouts#wide-default)
   - The Page Container will have a max width of 1536px, the content will be center aligned and white space will be displayed around the perimeter of the content.
2. [Narrow](https://design-system.hpe.design/templates/page-layouts#narrow) 
   - The PageContent will have a maximum width of 768px. On wider screens the content will be center aligned and white space will be displayed around the perimeter of the content.
3. [Full](https://design-system.hpe.design/templates/page-layouts#full)
   - This option will automatically resize the container to fill the screen displaying it. With the "full" option, if the screen grows wider than the available content, the Page Content will be left aligned to the edge of the screen.
